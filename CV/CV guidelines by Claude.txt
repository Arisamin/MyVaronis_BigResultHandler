## **The Modern CV Landscape: Two Critical Filters**

### **1. The ATS (Applicant Tracking System) Filter - The Robot Gatekeeper**

Around 98.4% of Fortune 500 companies use ATS scanners, and 75% of software resumes are rejected during the ATS screening process. This is your first hurdle.

**What ATS Systems Look For:**
- **Clean, parseable format**: Single-column layouts in reverse chronological order
- **Standard section headings**: "Work Experience," "Education," "Skills" (not creative alternatives)
- **Keywords matching the job description**: Technologies, programming languages, frameworks
- **Simple formatting**: No tables, text boxes, headers/footers, or graphics
- **Standard fonts**: Arial, Calibri, Helvetica (10-12pt)
- **File format**: PDF from Word/Google Docs (text must be highlightable)

**Why These Choices Matter:**
ATS software literally cannot "see" fancy designs. Decorative fonts won't impress anyone; they'll just make your resume harder to read (or worse, unreadable to the ATS). A beautifully designed CV with columns, graphics, or unusual fonts will be parsed as gibberish, automatically rejecting you before a human ever sees it.

### **2. The Human Filter - The 6-Second Scan**

FAANG recruiters spend only about 6 seconds (or less) scanning resumes. They'll do a short scan gathering all key information: location, years of experience, languages and technologies, position names, company names, and anything else that stands out.

**What Technical Recruiters Prioritize:**

1. **Quantifiable Impact** (Most Important)
   - Numbers, metrics, percentages showing your impact
   - Use numbers and quantify whatever you can: number of users, service load numbers, number of customer support tickets you proactively resolved
   - Example: "Reduced load time by 50%" beats "Improved performance"

2. **Technical Skills Match**
   - Immediate visibility of relevant technologies
   - Skills section should be at the top, after contact info
   - Both acronyms and full terms (e.g., "SEO (Search Engine Optimization)")

3. **Career Progression**
   - Clear growth trajectory from internships or junior roles moving up
   - Staying at positions for at least one year

4. **Relevant Experience**
   - Most recent and relevant roles first
   - Company names matter (well-known tech companies boost credibility)

## **The Winning Formula: Popular CV Formats for 2025**

### **Format #1: The "FAANG-Ready" Single-Column Resume**

**Structure (in this exact order):**
1. **Header**: Name, contact (phone, email, location, GitHub, LinkedIn)
2. **Professional Summary** (2-3 sentences highlighting years of experience, key technologies, notable achievements)
3. **Skills** (organized by category: Languages, Frameworks, Cloud, Tools, etc.)
4. **Work Experience** (reverse chronological, with quantified bullet points)
5. **Projects** (with GitHub links and metrics)
6. **Education** (degree, school, graduation year)
7. **Certifications** (if relevant)

**Why This Works:**
- The chronological format is widely considered the most ATS-friendly resume layout with clear, standard headings
- Puts skills front and center where both ATS and humans look first
- Your CV should be just one page long for those with less than 10 years of experience
- Single column allows easy top-to-bottom scanning

### **Format #2: The "Impact-First" Resume**

Similar structure but emphasizes:
- **Achievement-focused bullet points** using the formula: Action + Technology + Result
- Example: "Designed event-driven message handling using RabbitMQ, scaling to process 100TB per transaction with predictable throughput"

**Why This Works:**
- Highlighting a few of your best achievements is better than including many "average" achievements
- Directly addresses the recruiter's question: "What value will this person bring?"
- Recruiters want to see what will add value to the company, especially knowledge of common programming languages, frameworks, tools, and technologies

## **Critical Content Guidelines**

### **Professional Summary (2-3 sentences max):**
- State role, years of experience, core technologies, and one notable achievement
- Use action words and active voice with the noun that describes your role
- Example: "Senior Software Engineer with 5+ years designing distributed systems using C#, Azure, and RabbitMQ. Led migration to microservices architecture, reducing costs by 40%."

### **Work Experience Bullets:**
Use the **What-Why-Outcome** formula:
- **What**: "Designed and implemented event-driven message handling"
- **Technology**: "using RabbitMQ consumers and Azure Blob Storage"
- **Outcome**: "scaling to process up to 100TB per transaction with constant memory usage"

Write what and how you did things, not what a software engineer generally does.

### **Skills Section:**
- Organize by category (Languages, Frameworks, Cloud, DevOps, etc.)
- Include both the full term and its acronym to ensure the ATS picks up on it
- List skills relevant to the job description you're applying for

### **Projects:**
- Always link to GitHub or live demos
- Include metrics or impact (users, stars, performance improvements)
- Include at least 2 projects outlining key contributions, always linking the project name to GitHub

## **What NOT to Include:**

No fillers, personal details like birthdate, age, gender, marital status, religion, nationality, full native address, or salary expectations. Also avoid:
- Generic objective statements ("seeking a challenging position")
- Hobbies unless directly relevant and impressive
- Full employment history if not relevant
- GPA unless above 3.6/4.0 or 7.0/10.0

## **The Technical Details:**

### **File Format:**
- PDF format only, not Word or Google Docs
- Created from Word/Google Docs/LaTeX (never from Canva or graphic design tools)
- Text must be easily highlightable

### **Layout Specifics:**
- 0.5-inch margins on all sides
- No headers/footers (ATS can't read them)
- No columns, tables, or text boxes
- Consistent formatting (all bullets with or without periods, not mixed)

### **Length:**
- One page for <10 years experience
- Maximum two pages for >10 years experience

## **The Customization Secret:**

Tailor each resume to the specific job you're applying to. Do NOT use the same resume for every application.

**How to Customize:**
1. Copy job description into a word frequency analyzer
2. Identify key technologies and skills mentioned most
3. Incorporate those exact keywords naturally into your resume
4. Mirror the language used in the job description

## **Why These Choices Work:**

**From the HR/ATS Perspective:**
- Standardized formatting = reliable parsing = your data gets into their system correctly
- Keywords matching job description = high match score = you appear in their search results
- Clean structure = no errors = you pass automated screening

**From the Technical Recruiter Perspective:**
- Immediate visibility of technical skills = quick assessment of fit
- Quantified achievements = evidence of impact, not just responsibilities
- One-page format = respects their time (they're reviewing hundreds of CVs)
- GitHub links = ability to verify claims and see code quality
- Clear progression = demonstrates growth and stability

**The Bottom Line:**
Modern tech hiring is a two-stage game. Stage 1 is beating the robot (ATS) with clean formatting and keywords. Stage 2 is impressing the human with quantifiable impact and relevant experience in the 6 seconds they'll spend scanning your CV. The "best" format is the one that successfully passes both filters.
